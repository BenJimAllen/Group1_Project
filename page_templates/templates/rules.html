Rules

This is an example of a Sudoku game in progress.

A standard Sudoku game is played on a 9x9 grid, which is divided into nine 3x3 blocks of cells.

The numbers used in the game range from 1 to 9 (0 is not included).

This is an example of a solved Sudoku game.

The black numbers were provided at the beginning of the game, and the player filled in the blue numbers to solve the puzzle.

Note:

In a valid Sudoku solution, no number is repeated in any column, row, or 3x3 block!

Mistakes

(Greenspan and Lee)

The 5 is in the wrong spot!

There is already a 5 (highlighted in red) in the same column.

(Greenspan and Lee)

The 9 is in the wrong spot!

There is already a 9 (highlighted in red) in the same row.

(Greenspan and Lee)

The 1 is in the wrong spot!

There is already a 1 (highlighted in red) in the same 3x3 block.

(Greenspan and Lee)

Triple Trouble!

The 9 is in the wrong spot! It is repeated in the same column, row, and 3x3 block (all instances are highlighted in red).
